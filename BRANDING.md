# Branding Guidelines

## Name

The name **Vela** and the Vela logo are trademarks of Leon Nonnast.

## Allowed

- Use the name "Vela" to refer to this project in blog posts, articles, and discussions
- Fork and modify the code under the MIT License
- State that your project is "based on Vela" or "powered by Vela"

## Not Allowed

- Use the name "Vela" or the Vela logo for your own fork or derivative product in a way that suggests official endorsement
- Imply that your fork or product is the official Vela project
- Use the Vela logo in your own product branding without permission

## Forks and Derivatives

If you fork or create a derivative of Vela, you must:

1. Choose a **different name** for your project
2. Use your own logo and branding
3. Include attribution: "Based on [Vela](https://github.com/LeonNonnast/vela)" (or the applicable repository URL)

## Derivative Products

If you build a new product based on Vela, you must:

1. Use a **distinct product name** — not "Vela" or any name that could be confused with it
2. Include a **visible notice** in your product (e.g. About page, README, or documentation) stating:
   > This product is based on [Vela](https://github.com/LeonNonnast/vela), originally created by LeonNonnast.
3. Retain the original MIT License and copyright notice in all source files derived from Vela
4. Clearly distinguish your product from the original Vela project in all marketing and documentation

## Attribution

When using Vela in your projects, please include the following attribution where reasonable:

> Built with [Vela](https://github.com/LeonNonnast/vela)

## Questions

For branding questions or permission requests, please open an issue on the GitHub repository.
